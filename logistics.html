Venue, travel & accommodation information

📅
July is peak tourist season in Cambridge. We strongly recommend booking accommodation as early as possible. Availability near the venue can be limited.

🏠 Venue

The workshop takes place in the Ryle Room at the Kavli Institute for Cosmology Cambridge (KICC). KICC is located on the west side of Cambridge, on the grounds of the Institute of Astronomy (IoA), a short distance from the University's West Cambridge site.

Address:
Kavli Institute for Cosmology Cambridge (KICC)
Madingley Road, Cambridge CB3 0HA
United Kingdom

KICC shares its building with the Institute of Astronomy and is adjacent to the Cavendish Laboratory and the Institute for Theoretical Cosmology. Visitor parking is available on site (limited — cycling or public transport recommended).

✈️ Getting to Cambridge

By Train

  • From London King's Cross: direct trains to Cambridge (~50 min). Frequent service throughout the day.
  • From London Liverpool Street: direct trains to Cambridge (~1 hr 20 min). Also frequent.
  • From Cambridge station to KICC: taxi (~15 min, ~£12–15) or Stagecoach bus route 4 or Universal towards Madingley Road.

From London Airports

  • London Stansted (STN): Direct train to Cambridge (~30 min). The most convenient airport for Cambridge.
  • London Heathrow (LHR): Train to London Paddington / King's Cross, then train to Cambridge (~2 hr total). Or National Express coach direct to Cambridge (~3 hr, but no changes).
  • London Gatwick (LGW): Train to London Bridge or King's Cross, then to Cambridge (~2.5 hr total).
  • London Luton (LTN): Train to London St Pancras, then to Cambridge (~1.5 hr total). Luton Airport Parkway station is on the Thameslink line.

By Car

  • Cambridge is accessible via the M11 motorway from London (exit J13 for west Cambridge).
  • Parking in central Cambridge is very limited and expensive. KICC has limited visitor parking on-site; cycling from central accommodation is strongly preferred.
  • Park & Ride sites are available on the main routes into Cambridge.

🚲 Getting Around Cambridge

KICC is on the west side of Cambridge, approximately 2 miles from the city centre.

  • Cycling is the Cambridge way — highly recommended. Several hire schemes operate in the city (e.g. Cycling City, independent hire shops on the main roads).
  • Bus: Stagecoach routes 4 and Universal connect the city centre with Madingley Road / KICC regularly.
  • Taxi / rideshare: Uber operates in Cambridge; traditional black cabs are also available.
  • Walking: Approx. 35–40 min on foot from the city centre via Northampton Street and Madingley Road.

🏠 Accommodation

There is no block-booked accommodation for this workshop. Participants should book independently. Options near KICC:

Recommended

  • Hyatt Centric Cambridge — on Hills Road, near the train station. Modern hotel, excellent transport links to KICC via bus or cycle (~20 min). This is our top recommendation for workshop participants.

Other Hotels

  • Hotel Felix — Whitehouse Lane, Huntingdon Rd (~10 min walk from KICC). Boutique hotel in a Victorian villa. Excellent location for the workshop.
  • The Graduate Cambridge — Granta Place, city centre (~30 min cycle). 4-star hotel on the river Cam.
  • ibis Cambridge Central Station — Budget option near the train station (~20 min by bus).
  • The Lensfield Hotel — Lensfield Road (~25 min cycle). Mid-range, close to the city centre.

Other Options

  • Airbnb / private rentals: West Cambridge neighbourhoods (Newnham, Grantchester Road, Madingley Road) are very convenient and often excellent value for the period.

🇬🇧 General Information

  • Currency: British Pound Sterling (GBP). Cards are widely accepted everywhere in Cambridge.
  • Time zone: BST (UTC+1) in July.
  • Weather: July in Cambridge is generally warm (18–24°C) but changeable — a light waterproof layer is advisable.
  • Visa: Participants from outside the UK/EU should check entry requirements for the United Kingdom well in advance. An invitation letter can be provided on request — contact Nikhil Sarin.
  • Wi-Fi: Eduroam is available throughout KICC for participants with institutional accounts. Guest Wi-Fi will also be available.
  • Emergency: UK emergency services — dial 999.
